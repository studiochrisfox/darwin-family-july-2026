🔴
Book now — do not wait. July is peak season in Kakadu. Cooinda Lodge and Mercure Crocodile Hotel fill weeks ahead. Campgrounds book out. If you're visiting in early July, accommodation needs to be locked in today.
💡
The game plan: Base night 1–2 in Jabiru (northern Kakadu) for Ubirr and Nourlangie rock art, then drive south to Cooinda for nights 3–4 for Yellow Water, Maguk swimming and the Warradjan Cultural Centre. This covers the whole park without doubling back.
✓
Perfect for mixed ages. Kakadu has something for everyone — easy walks with big payoff, spectacular wildlife, world-class rock art, real swimming holes. Kids love the croc spottings. The Mercure Crocodile Hotel in Jabiru is shaped like a saltwater crocodile — kids are immediately obsessed.
Key highlights
🎨

Ubirr Rock Art + Sunset Escarpment

▾

One of Australia's finest accessible rock art galleries — X-ray fish, turtles, Mimi spirits, all painted over thousands of years by the Bininj/Mungguy people. The walk through the galleries takes about 45 minutes. Then climb up to the lookout for the single best sunset view in the Northern Territory — the Arnhem Land escarpment stretching away to the horizon, the floodplains turning golden. Arrive 1.5 hours before sunset to walk the art first. This is non-negotiable — do it on the first afternoon.

Distance from Jabiru: 40 km sealed. Walk: 1 km easy circuit. Cost: Included in park pass. Kids rating: ★★★★★ — Lewis and Reuben have done this and love it.

🐊

Yellow Water Billabong Cruise

▾

The iconic Kakadu experience. A 90-minute cruise on Yellow Water Billabong at dawn or dusk — saltwater crocodiles, jabiru storks, sea eagles, magpie geese by the thousands. The sunrise cruise is the best: wildlife at its most active, extraordinary light. Book the 6am or 6:30am departure. Book before you leave Darwin — this fills out weeks ahead.

Cost: ~$80 adult · ~$40 child (5–15). Location: Cooinda Lodge. Duration: 90 min. Kids absolutely love this — crocs right next to the boat.

🎨

Nourlangie (Burrungkuy) Rock Art

▾

The second great rock art site — includes Namarrgon (Lightning Man), one of Kakadu's most famous and significant paintings. The 1.5 km circuit walk is manageable for all ages. The rock formations themselves are spectacular. Do this on the way from Jabiru to Cooinda.

Distance from Jabiru: 30 km sealed. Cost: Included in park pass.

💧

Maguk (Barramundi Gorge) — swim

▾

A waterfall tumbling into a deep, clear pandanus-lined pool. One of the best swimming holes in Australia. The walk in (2 km return) is through monsoon forest — stunning. Croc-free (rangers confirm). Bring snorkels. Arrive before 9am to beat the crowd. 4WD and the Cub Escape can access this — it's 12 km unsealed from the Kakadu Highway.

Distance from Cooinda: 25 km (12 km unsealed). Cost: Included in park pass. Kids' favourite swim in Kakadu.

🌀

Warradjan Aboriginal Cultural Centre

▾

Right next to Cooinda Lodge — free with park pass entry. Excellent interactive displays developed by the Bininj/Mungguy traditional owners. 65,000 years of human history brought to life. Saturday and Sunday morning free workshops — weaving, damper making, bush food stories with traditional owners. For the extended family group, this is one of the most meaningful 90 minutes in Kakadu.

Cost: Free with Kakadu Park Pass. Open daily 9am–5pm.

💧

Gunlom (Waterfall Creek) — infinity pools

▾

The most photogenic spot in Kakadu — tiered waterfall into a plunge pool, and infinity pools at the top with views across the escarpment. The upper pool walk is 2.8 km return and worth every step. If doing a 4th night, base at Mary River Roadhouse for Gunlom access. 33 km unsealed from Kakadu Highway. Croc-free. Arrive early.

Distance from Cooinda: ~45 km (33 km unsealed). 4WD required. Cost: included in park pass.

Katherine Gorge — Nitmiluk

2 nights from Darwin. 300 km sealed. No permits. One of the NT's great natural wonders — and easy from Nakara.

300

13

🏞
What it is: Nitmiluk (Katherine Gorge) is 13 interconnected sandstone gorges carved by the Katherine River through ancient escarpment country. In July the water levels are perfect — deep enough to swim and canoe, not flooded. The Jawoyn people's Country. Managed jointly by Jawoyn and Parks Australia.
✓
Why it's perfect for the extended family. Katherine has genuinely good infrastructure — Nitmiluk Visitor Centre, guided canoe hire, the gorge cruise, and the town of Katherine itself with a supermarket, good pub and restaurants. Mixed fitness levels are handled easily — you can do a gentle boat cruise or a full 2-gorge walk, depending on the group.
Key highlights
🛶

Canoe the Gorge

▾

The classic Katherine Gorge experience — paddle through towering sandstone walls into the second gorge, portage over rocks between gorges, swim in the pools. Half-day or full-day hire available. Guides available. Lewis and Reuben have done this before and loved it. For the extended family, canoe hire can accommodate large groups — book well ahead in July.

Single canoes and double canoes available. Lifejackets provided. The portage between gorges 1 and 2 is a 5-minute scramble over boulders — manageable for all ages but slightly challenging for very young children. Freshwater crocodiles are common — visible basking on rocks. They are harmless.

Cost: ~$50/half day double canoe · ~$80/full day. Book: nitmiluk.com.au

🚢

Gorge Cruise

▾

For family members who prefer not to paddle — the 2-hour gorge cruise takes you through the first two gorges by flat-bottomed boat. Excellent commentary on geology and Jawoyn culture. Good option for grandparents, young kids and those who want the gorge experience without the physical effort.

Cost: ~$55 adult · ~$30 child. Book: nitmiluk.com.au

🥾

Two-Gorge Walk

▾

8.4 km return. Moderate. For the fitter members of the group — crosses between gorge 1 and 2 on foot, with spectacular views of the escarpment and swimming in the gorge pools. Lewis and Reuben are well capable of this. Start early (by 7am) to beat the heat. Takes about 3.5 hours return. Bring plenty of water.

Cost: Free with park entry. Best for: Anyone reasonably fit — not suitable for small children or those with mobility issues.

💧

Edith Falls (Leliyn)

▾

60 km north of Katherine on the Stuart Highway — a beautiful tiered waterfall with a large croc-free swimming pool at the base. One of the best natural swimming holes in the NT. Easy 2.6 km walk to the upper pool for a second, quieter swim. Perfect for a hot afternoon on the way home. Stop here on the return trip to Darwin.

Cost: Included in Nitmiluk park pass. Road: Sealed all the way, 2WD accessible.

🎨

Nitmiluk Visitor Centre

▾

Excellent centre presenting Jawoyn culture and the park's geology. Free with park pass. Spend an hour here before heading to the gorge — it meaningfully enriches the whole experience. The Jawoyn connection to the gorge system (Nitmiluk means "cicada dreaming place") is a great entry point for the extended family to understand what they're visiting.

Cost: Free with park entry. Open daily 7am–7pm.

2-Night Katherine Itinerary

From Nakara. All sealed road. Suggested dates: 8–10 July or 15–17 July.

📅
Sequencing with Kakadu: Do Kakadu first (4–7 July), then Katherine (8–10 July) — or the other way around. Both are easy day drives from Darwin. They don't overlap in what they offer, so the order doesn't matter much.
D1
Day 1 · Morning departure · ~3.5 hrs
Nakara → Katherine → Nitmiluk
Depart Nakara by 8am. Stuart Highway south. Arrive Katherine by 11:30am — supermarket shop and lunch in town. Drive 30 km east to Nitmiluk Visitor Centre — orientation, park passes if not already purchased, Jawoyn cultural displays. Afternoon: gorge swimming at the boat ramp area — the clear water with the sandstone walls is immediately stunning. Book canoes for tomorrow at the visitor centre. Check in to Nitmiluk Caravan Park or Katherine town accommodation. Campfire or pub dinner.
Gorge afternoon swimNitmiluk Visitor CentreBook canoes for D2~3.5 hrs Nakara → Nitmiluk
D2
Day 2 · Big gorge day
Canoes + Gorge + Swim + Walk
Early start — canoes by 8am before it gets warm. Split the group: fitter adults and kids (Lewis, Reuben and others) take canoes into the gorge; grandparents or those preferring comfort do the 2-hour gorge cruise. Both are excellent. Canoeists paddle into the second gorge for a swim in the deep pools. Portage between gorges takes 5 mins. Regroup for lunch at the visitor centre. Afternoon: fitter members do the Baruwei Loop (5.4 km) or the start of the Two-Gorge Walk if legs are good. Sunset drinks at the gorge lookout.

D3
Day 3 · Return via Edith Falls
Slow morning → Edith Falls → Nakara
Slow gorge morning — last swim, pack up. Drive north on Stuart Highway, turn off to Edith Falls (Leliyn) — 60 km from Katherine, sealed road all the way. The swimming hole at the base is one of the NT's best — croc-free, clear water, easy short walk. Upper pool walk (2.6 km) for those who want it. Lunch at the falls. Then home to Nakara — arrive late afternoon. Two nights, three remarkable gorge experiences, and a family memory that'll last.
